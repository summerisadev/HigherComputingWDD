Aretha Franklin

After her mother's death, Aretha began singing solos at New Bethel. When she was 12 her father began managing her career, helping her sign her first recording deal with J.V.B. Records. Her first album, Songs of Faith, was released in 1956. Dinah Washington said at the time that "Aretha was the next one".

Image of the Aretha Franklin street sign

Aretha has 20 number 1 records in America. Her first was "I Never Loved a Man (The Way I Love You)" in 1967 and her last was "Freeway of Love" in 1985. Stand out records between were "Respect", "Chain of Fools" and "Think" which she also sang in The Blues Brother film.
